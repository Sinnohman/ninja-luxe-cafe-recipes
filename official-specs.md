## 7. GRINDER & GRIND SETTINGS

### Specifications
- **Type:** Integrated Conical Burr Grinder
- **Settings:** 1 (finest) to 25 (coarsest)
- **Barista Assist Technology™:** Automatically recommends grind size based on beans and previous brew output
- **Recommended display:** Numbers on left side of control panel show recommended grind; match with physical grind dial on side

### Grind Size Guidance
- **Coarser settings (higher numbers):** Better for coffee drinks
- **Finer settings (lower numbers):** Better for espresso drinks
- Match CURRENT grind (dial) to RECOMMENDED grind (display)

### Dialing In
- When using new beans: 2–3 brews to reach optimal grind setting
- Over time: Recommended grind adjusts to account for aging beans (normal behavior)
- Changing from very coarse to very fine (or vice versa): Grinder may cycle briefly to prevent jamming

### Flavor Adjustment
- **Less Bitter:** Decrease grind size (finer)
- **Less Sour:** Increase grind size (coarser)

### Grinder Jam
- Can occur with light roasts at too fine a setting
- Clear: Remove/empty hopper, set grind to 25, press & hold START GRIND with portafilter in cradle

### Grinder Clog
- Buildup of residue from oily/dark roast beans
- Clear: Unplug machine, clean grind chute with brush

### Bean Notes
- DO NOT add pre-ground beans to hopper (whole beans only)
- DO NOT use cold/frozen beans
- Avoid flavored beans (added oils/sugars increase residue)
- Clean grinder weekly for daily use; more often for dark roast/decaf/oily beans

## 9. TAMPING

### When to Tamp
- **ALWAYS tamp:** All espresso drinks + 16 oz. and 18 oz. coffee drinks
- **Level only (no tamp):** 6 oz. to 14 oz. coffee drinks — lightly shake portafilter instead

### Tamping Procedure
1. Remove portafilter from grinding cradle (keep funnel attached)
2. Place portafilter on solid, level surface (use towel on delicate countertops)
3. Place assisted tamper inside funnel
4. Press tamper ALL the way down until it contacts the funnel
5. Remove tamper and funnel; store both in machine's side compartments
6. **For quad shots only:** Tamp normally with funnel, then remove funnel and tamp one more time

## 10. BREWING PROCEDURE

### Espresso / Coffee Brewing
1. Select basket → insert into portafilter → add funnel
2. Insert portafilter+funnel into grinding cradle (level, fully seated)
3. Machine auto-detects basket and illuminates compatible brew options
4. Turn center dial to select desired brew style/size
5. Match grind dial to recommended grind setting
6. Press START GRIND → machine grinds → beeps → displays TAMP
7. Tamp as described above
8. Insert portafilter into group head (align orange dots, twist to lock arrow)
9. Place cup underneath (use adjustable tray for small cups; remove center drip tray for tall mugs)
10. Press START BREW → progress bar illuminates → beeps when done

### Proper Extraction Indicators
- Drink pours at slow, consistent rate
- Caramel-colored liquid (crema) visible during pour
- Layer of crema on top of finished drink

## 11. FROTHING

### Froth Styles
| Style | Description | Heated? |
|---|---|---|
| **Steamed Milk** | Gently heated without whisking. Great for coffee. | Yes |
| **Thin Froth** | Thin layer of foam for lattes. | Yes |
| **Thick Froth** | Classic fluffy froth for cappuccinos. | Yes |
| **Extra-Thick Froth*** | Large layer of froth, less volume. | Yes |
| **Cold Foam** | Cold froth for iced drinks (snowflake icon). | No |

*Not available on all models.

### Frothing Procedure (Automatic)
1. Fill milk jug between min/max lines (whisk installed)
2. Ensure bottom of jug is dry; place on milk jug platform with wand inserted
3. Turn froth dial to desired style
4. Press MILK TYPE to select DAIRY or PLANT-BASED
5. Press START FROTH → progress bar shows status → beeps when done
6. After frothing: immediately wipe wand with damp cloth before pushing into down position
7. Wand auto-purges when pushed down; tap and swirl jug before pouring

### Manual Frothing
1. Fill jug, select steamed milk setting
2. Pull wand out, place in jug spout, angle slightly
3. Hold START FROTH for 3 seconds → steam generates
4. Place wand just below milk surface to stretch (incorporate air, create whirlpool)
5. Once volume is right, move jug up (wand deeper) to heat
6. When jug is too hot to hold, press START FROTH to stop

### Froth Queuing
- While brewing, you can queue froth: set milk type + froth style → press START FROTH
- Cold foam can froth simultaneously with brewing (no need to queue)

### Best Frothing Practices
- Use whole milk for dairy; barista/full-fat for plant-based
- Use cold milk (from refrigerator)
- Ensure milk jug is room-temperature before frothing
- UHT-treated milks may have varying results
- Coconut milk heats but won't produce stable froth
- Clean wand immediately after each use
- Deep clean whisk: fill jug to max with water + drop of dish soap, run THIN FROTH

## 13. BREW TEMPERATURE ADJUSTMENT (Quick Access via h06)

1. Hold SIZE for 3 seconds
2. Rotate center dial to _h06, press to select
3. Rotate to LO / M / HI, press to confirm
4. Press SIZE to exit

**Pro tip:** Run a water flush (CLASSIC, 12 oz.) before brewing to preheat group head and portafilter. For even hotter drinks: run a water-only double shot through portafilter into your cup, discard, dry, then brew.

---

## 14. CHANGING / SWITCHING BEANS

1. Unlock hopper (rotate handle counterclockwise)
2. Remove hopper, pour out remaining beans
3. Replace empty hopper, lock (clockwise)
4. Insert portafilter+funnel in grinding cradle
5. Press and hold START GRIND to purge residual grounds
6. Add new beans to hopper
7. For best results, reset Barista Assist (h04) before dialing in new beans

## 15. CLEANING & MAINTENANCE

### Immediately After Brew
- Remove portafilter, knock out grounds
- Remove basket, hand-wash with soapy water, dry
- Wipe steam wand with damp cloth, push down to auto-purge
- Empty drip tray when orange indicator visible

### Drip Tray
- Holds bypass water from ~6 cappuccinos
- Water bypass during brew and wand purge after frothing is NORMAL
- Remove main drip tray from machine, pour out through spout
- Remove center drip tray before emptying; wipe separately

### Cleaning the Conical Burr Grinder
**Frequency:** Weekly minimum for daily use. More often for dark roast/decaf/oily beans. Monthly for occasional use.

1. Unplug machine. Remove all beans. Remove hopper.
2. Remove upper burr: rotate counterclockwise, align orange arrows, lift out
3. Clean removed burr with brush
4. Clean fixed burr with brush
5. Clean inside of grinding cradle with brush
6. Reinstall burr
7. **DO NOT let burrs contact water**

### Cleaning the Steam Wand
1. Remove cap from cleaning brush to access needle
2. Use needle to clean steam wand tip
3. For deep clean: use wrench (middle of brush) to remove wand tip
4. Use needle to clean inside of wand
5. After cleaning, run a purge

### Shower Screen & Rubber Gasket
- If brew quality declines or leaking at portafilter: remove and clean shower screen
- Use Phillips-head screwdriver to remove central screw
- Clean with soap and water
- Inspect rubber gasket; replace if worn (available at ninjakitchen.com/accessories)
- Reinstall shower screen, secure with screw

### Descaling Cycle
**Trigger:** DESCALE light illuminates (approx. 90 minutes)
**IMPORTANT:** If not descaled after light turns on, drink quality will diminish and machine may clog/stop working.

**Procedure:**
1. Empty main drip tray completely
2. **Option A:** Dissolve provided Ninja descale powder in 37 fl. oz. water → pour into reservoir
3. **Option B:** Fill reservoir with 16 oz. white vinegar, then fill to descale line with water
4. Insert double basket in portafilter → fasten in group head
5. Place milk jug on platform with wand inserted
6. Place empty cup (≥18 oz.) under portafilter
7. Press DESCALE → press center dial to start
8. Machine flushes through wand and portafilter for ~90 minutes (countdown displayed)
9. After completion: remove portafilter, drip tray, milk jug carefully (all contain water)
10. Hand-wash all with warm soapy water
11. Refill reservoir with fresh water to Max Fill
12. Reinstall drip tray and portafilter (NOT milk jug)
13. Flush group head: run 12 oz. CLASSIC brew
14. Flush wand: press PURGE (milk jug uninstalled)

**NOTE:** Remove water filter before descaling (sold separately).

### Clean Cycle
**Trigger:** CLEAN light illuminates (approx. 6 minutes)
**Requires:** Cleaning disc + 1 cleaning tablet (included)

1. Empty main drip tray completely (clean cycle water goes there)
2. Fill reservoir to Max Fill
3. Insert double basket in portafilter
4. Place cleaning disc on basket (handle facing up)
5. Place 1 cleaning tablet in center of disc
6. Fasten portafilter in group head
7. Press CLEAN → press center dial to start
8. Machine backflushes shower screen and group head (~6 min)
9. After completion: carefully remove portafilter and drip tray (both may have water)
10. Hand-wash with warm soapy water

## 16. RINSE NOTIFICATION

If brewing a cold drink immediately after a hot drink, machine displays RINSE. This is to cool the system.

1. Remove portafilter from group head
2. Place ≥12 oz. cup under group head
3. Press START BREW — machine rinses ~8 oz. water
4. Dispose of water
5. **To avoid RINSE:** brew all cold drinks before hot drinks

## 17. FIRST USE SETUP

### Water Hardness Test
1. Remove test strip from pack
2. Submerge in brewing water for 5–10 seconds
3. Remove, lightly shake
4. Place on dry, flat surface; wait 1 minute
5. Count purple squares (0–5)
6. Plug in machine; display shows hd 5 initially
7. Rotate center dial to match strip result (e.g., hd 1 for 1 purple square)
8. Press center dial to confirm
9. To reset later: Additional Settings → _h08

### Water Flush (Before First Brew)
1. Double basket installed, portafilter in group head (align orange dots, twist to lock arrow)
2. Turn center dial to CLASSIC
3. Press SIZE → 12 oz.
4. Place ≥14 oz. cup
5. Press START BREW → dispose water, wash cup

## 19. TROUBLESHOOTING SUMMARY

### No Water / No Espresso Flow
- Reservoir filled? (Luxe basket needs ≥18 oz.)
- Reservoir firmly seated?
- Machine warming up?
- Grind too fine? Follow recommended setting
- Needs descaling?

### Espresso Stream Too Slow
- Grind too fine → follow recommended
- Basket clogged → clean before grinding
- Needs descaling

### Espresso Stream Too Fast
- Grind too coarse → follow recommended
- Tamped too lightly → push tamper until it contacts funnel

### Espresso Runs Around Portafilter Edge
- Portafilter not aligned (orange dots + lock arrow)
- Grounds on basket rim → clean edges
- Group head seal needs replacement → call 1-877-646-5288

### Frothing Won't Start
- Machine not at temperature → wait
- Low/no water in reservoir
- Reservoir not seated
- Wand not in jug / jug not on platform
- Milk below min line

### Coffee Not Hot Enough
- Preheat portafilter and cup with hot water
- Adjust brew temp via h06 (LO/M/HI)
- Use heated froth (cold foam is not heated)
- Needs descaling

### No Crema
- Tamped too lightly → press tamper firmly to funnel
- Grind too coarse
- Beans not fresh → use within 20 days of "Roasted On" date
- Basket holes clogged

### Drip Tray Fills Quickly
- Normal: water bypass during brew and wand purge after frothing
- Holds water from ~6 cappuccinos
- Empty when orange indicator visible

### Drink Too Bitter/Acidic
- Match grind setting to recommended
- Adjust ratio via Additional Settings
- Temperature: Low/Medium for dark roasts, Medium/High for light roasts

### Grinder Jammed
- Light roast at too fine setting
- Clear: remove hopper, set grind to 25, press & hold START GRIND

### Grinder Clogged
- Oily bean residue buildup
- Clear: unplug, clean grind chute with brush

## 22. NOTES FOR WEBSITE VERIFICATION

- **Temperature:** Three settings (LO/M/HI), not specific degrees. Controlled via h06.
- **Froth Temperature:** Controlled via h07 (LO/M/HI), separate from brew temp.
- **Grind Settings:** 25 discrete steps, not continuously variable. Grind dial is physical, on the side.
- **Strength:** Espresso = water-to-grounds ratio (4:1 / 2.5:1 / 2:1). Coffee = dose adjustment (Mild/Default/Bold).
- **Auto Shut-Off:** 20 minutes of inactivity.
- **No explicit "h00" setting** — the additional settings menu starts at _h01.
- **Descaling:** ~90 minutes. Uses Ninja descale powder (37 oz water) OR white vinegar (16 oz + water to descale line).
- **Clean Cycle:** ~6 minutes. Uses cleaning disc + 1 tablet.
- **Rinse Notification:** ~8 oz. water flush when switching from hot to cold brewing.
