## 3. CONTROL PANEL LAYOUT

The control panel is divided into three sections:

### Left Section — Grind Control
- **POWER Button (A):** Turns machine on/off
- **START GRIND Button (B):** Initiates grinding process
- **Progress Bar (C):** Shows progress of brewing or grinding
- **Current Grind Setting Display:** Shows the actual grind dial position
- **Recommended Grind Setting Display:** Barista Assist Technology™ recommendation

### Middle Section — Brew Control
- **Center Dial / START BREW Button (I):** Selects brew options and starts brewing
- **Espresso Options (left side):** Espresso, Quad, Cold-Pressed
- **Coffee Options (right side):** Classic, Rich, Over Ice, Cold Brew
- **SIZE Selection Button (H):** Adjusts drink size (6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18 oz)
- **STRENGTH Button (J):** Adjusts brew strength
- **DESCALE Button (K):** Starts descaling cycle (illuminates when needed)
- **CLEAN Button (F):** Starts cleaning cycle (illuminates when needed)

### Right Section — Froth Control
- **Froth Dial / START FROTH Button (D):** Selects froth style and starts frothing
  - Steamed Milk
  - Thin Froth
  - Thick Froth
  - Cold Foam (denoted with snowflake ❄️)
- **MILK TYPE Button (G):** Selects Dairy or Plant-Based milk
- **PURGE Button (E):** Purges the steam wand

## 8. GRINDER SPECIFICATIONS & SETTINGS

### Grinder Details
- **Type:** Conical burr grinder
- **Settings:** 25 levels
- **Scale:** 1 = finest, 25 = coarsest
- **Coarser settings (higher numbers):** Better for coffee/drip drinks
- **Finer settings (lower numbers):** Better for espresso drinks
- **Barista Assist Technology™:** Machine recommends grind setting based on bean type and drink selection
- **Dosing:** Weight-based with built-in scale

### Understanding Grind Settings
- Matching the recommended setting brings the best flavors out of chosen beans
- The machine adjusts based on what beans are being used to prevent over-extraction or under-extraction
- For dark-roast beans: use low or medium temperature
- For medium or light-roast beans: use medium or high temperature

### Switching Out Beans
1. Unlock bean hopper by rotating handle counterclockwise
2. Remove hopper and pour out leftover beans
3. Place empty hopper back, lock it
4. Insert portafilter with funnel into grinding cradle
5. Press and hold START GRIND to purge residual grounds
6. Add new beans

### Grinder Troubleshooting
- If ADD BEANS appears despite beans present: unplug machine, gently push beans toward burrs
- Clean the grinding cradle with the brush periodically

## 10. FROTH STYLES & MILK FROTHING

### Froth Presets
| Froth Style | Description |
|---|---|
| Steamed Milk | Hot, silky microfoam |
| Thin Froth | Light, airy foam |
| Thick Froth | Dense, creamy foam (cappuccino-style) |
| Cold Foam ❄️ | Classic layer of cold froth (not heated) |

### Milk Type Selection
- **DAIRY** — for cow's milk
- **PLANT-BASED** — for almond, oat, soy, etc.

### Frothing Procedure
1. Fill milk jug with desired amount (do not exceed max fill line)
2. Place jug on milk jug platform with steam wand inside
3. Select DAIRY or PLANT-BASED milk with MILK TYPE button
4. Turn froth dial to select froth type
5. Press START FROTH — machine beeps at start and completion
6. Progress bar illuminates to show frothing status
7. After frothing, immediately wipe steam wand with damp cloth before pushing down
8. When wand is pushed back to down position, auto-purging occurs

### Froth Queuing
- After brew has started, froth can be queued to start immediately after brew finishes
- Select milk type and froth setting while brewing, then press START FROTH
- Cold foam does NOT need to be queued — it can froth simultaneously while brewing

### Manual Frothing
1. Move jug up so steam wand is further below the surface to heat
2. Stay in position until jug is too hot to hold
3. Press START FROTH to turn off

### Important Frothing Notes
- DO NOT froth without main drip tray inserted
- DO NOT froth milk directly into espresso/coffee — always froth in milk jug and pour
- Cold Foam mode does not heat
- Froth temperature can be adjusted in Additional Settings (h07)
- Cannot grind and froth simultaneously

## 12. CLEANING & MAINTENANCE

### Immediate After-Brew Cleaning
1. When brew is complete, carefully remove portafilter from group head
2. Dispose of grounds by tapping basket surface over a container or knock box
3. Rinse portafilter and basket

### Emptying the Drip Tray
- Drip tray fills during brewing (normal)
- Holds residual water from ~6 cappuccinos
- Empty when orange indicator rises enough to be visible
- Remove tray by pulling away, pour water through pour spout
- After brewing coffee, a significant amount of water in drip tray is normal

### Cleaning the Steam Wand
1. After each use: wipe wand with damp cloth before pushing down
2. Auto-purge occurs when wand is pushed to down position
3. Deep clean: remove tip with hex key (on cleaning brush), use needle to unclog residues
4. Ensure rubber seal is clean

### Removing the Shower Screen & Replacing Rubber Gasket
1. Machine must be turned off and unplugged
2. Remove shower screen
3. Clean with soap and water
4. Replace rubber gasket as needed

### Cleaning the Conical Burr Grinder
1. Turn off and unplug machine
2. Remove bean hopper
3. Clean burr grinder with brush
4. Over time, material can build up — clean periodically

### Cleaning Cycle (CLEAN Button)
- When CLEAN button illuminates, run cleaning cycle
- Use provided cleaning disc and cleaning tablets
- Follow on-screen/intelligent notification prompts

### Dishwasher-Safe Parts
- Funnel
- Whisk
- Water tank
- Bean hopper
- Drip tray

## 13. DESCALING PROCEDURE

**Time to complete:** Approximately 90 minutes

### When to Descale
- DESCALE light/button illuminates
- Machine needs descaling to maintain proper temperature and extraction

### US Model Procedure
1. Empty drip tray
2. Dissolve provided Ninja descale powder in separate container with 37 fl. oz. of water
3. Pour solution mixture into water reservoir
4. Insert double basket into portafilter
5. Place pitcher under steam wand
6. Press DESCALE and confirm
7. Cycle runs automatically (~90 minutes)
8. After cycle completes, rinse with clean water

### EU/UK Model Procedure
1. DESCALE indicator lights up
2. Empty drip tray
3. Dissolve Ninja descaling powder in 1 L of water
   - Alternative: 500 ml white vinegar + water up to Descale line
4. Insert double basket into portafilter
5. Place pitcher under steam wand
6. Press DESCALE and confirm
7. Cycle lasts about 90 minutes
8. Rinse with clean water

## 14. TROUBLESHOOTING & FAQ

### Espresso Not Flowing
- Check water tank is sufficiently filled (min. 50 cl for Luxe basket) and properly attached
- Grind may be too fine — use recommended setting
- If persists, run descaling cycle

### Espresso Flowing Too Slowly
- Grind too fine or excessive tamping — use recommended grind setting
- Tamp until tamper touches funnel (not harder)
- Clean basket holes
- Descale machine if needed

### No Crema on Espresso
- Use beans roasted less than 20 days ago
- Ensure grind is not too coarse
- Clean the basket
- Proper tamping improves crema

### Drink Not Hot Enough
- Preheat cup and portafilter with hot water
- Adjust brew temperature via settings menu (hold SIZE 3s → h06 → LO/M/HI)
- Use thin, appropriately sized cup
- If persists, descale the machine

### Grinder Not Grinding
- Check hopper contains beans and is properly installed
- Ensure portafilter with funnel is correctly inserted in grinding cradle
- If ADD BEANS appears despite beans: unplug, gently push beans toward burrs
- Clean grinding cradle with brush

### Drip Tray Fills Quickly
- Normal: machine purges water from steam wand after each frothing
- Releases a little water during brewing to maintain pressure
- Holds residual water from about 6 cappuccinos
- Empty when orange indicator appears

### Cold Drink After Hot Brew
- Run a Rinse cycle (follow Brew Tips instructions)
- RINSE notification will appear

### Can I Grind and Froth Simultaneously?
- **No.** You cannot grind and froth at the same time.

### Water in Drip Tray After Brewing Coffee
- Normal. During/after brew, small amount of water dispensed into drip tray
- Empty frequently or when orange indicator is visible

### How to Adjust Grinder Setting
- Use grind dial on left side of machine
- Match current grind size to recommended grind size on control panel

## 15. BEST BREWING & FROTHING PRACTICES

### Brewing Best Practices
- Use freshly roasted coffee beans for best results
- Preheat portafilter: rinse under hot water before grinding (ensure basket is completely dry before grinding)
- Preheat cup: rinse under hot water before brewing
- Match grind setting: use grind dial to match current grind to recommended setting
- Tamp on all espresso drinks: place portafilter on level surface, place tamper in funnel, push until contact
- Proper extraction: drink pours out at a slow, consistent rate
- For dark-roast beans: low or medium brew temperature
- For medium/light-roast beans: medium or high brew temperature

### Frothing Best Practices
- Fill milk jug with desired amount (do not exceed max fill line)
- Select correct milk type (DAIRY or PLANT-BASED)
- All froth settings are heated EXCEPT cold foam (❄️)
- Wipe steam wand immediately after each use
- DO NOT froth milk directly into drink — froth in jug, then pour
